About

Background

PhD Candidate in Anthropology at the University of Alabama, specializing in Maya archaeology with expertise in ceramic analysis, epigraphy, and regional artistic styles. My research bridges art historical methodology with archaeological practice to reconstruct ancient Maya political and social contexts from both excavated and looted ceramic collections.

Research

Saxche Orange Polychromes and Regional Style Development

Investigating the emergence and evolution of regional ceramic styles during the Early-Late Classic transition at Maya sites in the Holmul region.

Methodology: Formal analysis, Paleographic scrutiny, Comparative iconography

Status: Ongoing

Political Identity and Cultural Negotiation at La Sufricaya

Examining how political and cultural identities were negotiated in public and residential contexts during the Classic Maya transition period.

Teaching Philosophy

I teach with an inquiry-based, constructivist approach that keeps evidence at the center. Students form durable understanding when they test ideas against what they already know, confront gaps or doubts in the record, and revise their claims with clear criteria in view. This produces flexible thinkers who can adjust when new data or methods appear, rather than repeat conclusions that no longer fit. I plan courses with outcomes set first and align readings and assessments to those ends.
